# Customer Persona: Jake "The Outfitter" Morrison

## Demographics

- **Age:** 42
- **Location:** Western Montana
- **Role:** Owner/Operator of Big Sky Outfitters
- **Experience:** 18 years in the outfitting business
- **Education:** Bachelor's in Wildlife Management
- **Family:** Married with two kids (ages 14 and 16)

## Business Profile

- **Company Size:** 4 full-time staff, 6 seasonal guides
- **Annual Hunts:** 180-220 guided hunts per year
- **Hunt Types:** Elk, mule deer, whitetail, black bear, mountain lion
- **Properties:** Leases on 45,000 acres across 3 ranches
- **Repeat Client Rate:** 65%
- **Average Hunt Package:** $4,500
- **Peak Season:** September - November

## Daily Responsibilities

- Coordinating guide assignments and schedules
- Responding to booking inquiries via phone, email, and Facebook
- Managing client expectations and hunt preparations
- Maintaining relationships with landowners
- Ensuring regulatory compliance and licensing
- Handling payment collections and deposits
- Leading select premium hunts personally
- Equipment and property maintenance

## Pain Points

### Current Workflow Challenges
1. **Scattered Information:** Client data across Excel, email, handwritten notes, and memory
2. **Double Bookings:** Has accidentally double-booked guides twice this season
3. **Payment Tracking:** Chasing down final payments takes 5-10 hours per week
4. **Communication Chaos:** Misses inquiries that come through different channels
5. **Compliance Stress:** Constantly worried about missing permit renewals or harvest reporting deadlines
6. **Limited Growth:** Can't scale beyond current capacity without better systems

### Specific Frustrations
- "I spend more time on paperwork than I do in the field"
- "I know I'm losing repeat bookings because I don't follow up systematically"
- "My guides can't access client preferences when they're at camp"
- "I have three separate systems for payments, scheduling, and client info"
- "Every spring I rebuild my spreadsheets from scratch"

### Business Goals

### Personal Goals

## Decision-Making Process

1. **Awareness:** Hears about solution from another outfitter or sees ad
2. **Research:** Visits website, watches demo video, reads reviews
3. **Evaluation:** Signs up for free trial, tests during off-season
4. **Consultation:** Wants to talk to someone who understands hunting business
5. **Trial:** Runs it parallel with old system for 1-2 months
6. **Decision:** Commits if it proves time savings and ease of use
